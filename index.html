Comprising interdisciplinary researchers from Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) at IIT Bombay, our team has rigorously delved into the complexities of the onion value chain for over 5 years. Leveraging advanced technical and research skills, we are actively engaged in pioneering innovations within the realm of agricultural value chains. Our primary focus lies in developing cutting-edge, end-to-end solutions designed to enhance efficiency and affordability while minimizing waste. Through meticulous analysis and implementation of technical strategies, we aim to significantly augment the income prospects for farmers. Join us as we navigate the intricate intersections of technology and agriculture, pushing the boundaries of what's possible for a sustainable and optimized agricultural future!

Detailed analysis of the Onion value chain in India started in 2018 as a part of field research at CTARA. Later a small prototype of 3 MT was developed and tested for evaluating the technical feasibility of the intervention. Upon receiving the promising results in 2019, more sophisticated and optimised new designs were carried out for better performance and techno-economic feasibility. (2019-2022). Three such modular storage structures are now operational and real time data is receiving over the server for the purpose of experimentation, research, control and commercial aspects. In 2021, for PoCRA (Project on Climate Resilient Agriculture - Govt of Maharashtra and World Bank supported initiative), we prepared a Detailed Project report for implementing 300 MT CAOSS Facility with selected FPCs. In 2022, 2 such modular retrofitted storage structures were created at farmgate with fund support from BAIF and RuTAG (IIT Bombay) respectively. In same year, design and installation of Automated CAOSS of 100 MT capacity started at Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Krishi Vidyapeeth started. It will be operational by the ne dof 2o23.
